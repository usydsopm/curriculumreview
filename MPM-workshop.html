🗣 Workshop discussion — overall curriculum structure

The workshop identified several structural issues in the current MPM curriculum, particularly around repeated unit offerings, staff capacity, unit sequencing, and student enrolment pathways.

A key theme was the need to streamline the degree structure so that students can more easily enrol in the correct units, while also ensuring any changes continue to support Graduate Certificate and Graduate Diploma pathways.

There was general support for reducing repeated offerings where possible. Apart from Professional Project Practice (PMGT5205) and Capstone (PMGT5850), the proposal discussed was to offer most other core units once per year. This would help address staff capacity issues and improve curriculum planning.

The group also noted that the current content does not appear to be strongly scaffolded. Further work is needed to determine whether the content should be delivered in a more intentional sequence, and how this would compare with one-year and two-year programs at other universities.

    • Min 6 / max 18 cp of Technical electives

    • Min 6 / max 18 cp of Leadership electives
    Research pathway — 18 cp thesis + 12 cp electives
    • 18 cp of Thesis units
    • 6 cp of Strategic elective units
    • 6 cp of Leadership elective units

    02 When units run

    Unit availability

    A record of when units are currently offered across the academic year, and the changes to core and elective availability proposed at the workshop.

    a Core units · expected every semester
    🗣 Workshop discussion — core unit availability

    The group proposed changing several core units to one semester of availability per year:

    • PMGT5858 — Project Initiation
    • PMGT5811 — Critical and Systems Thinking
    • PMGT5820 — Execution and Acceptance
    • PMGT5871 — Project Planning
    • PMGT5872 — People and Communications

    This change could support more efficient staffing and unit delivery. However, the group noted that timetabling constraints may arise, as fewer offerings would result in larger class sizes. Venue availability may therefore need to be reviewed before progressing this change.

    There was also discussion about keeping PMGT5872 available in both semesters rather than moving it to a single semester, given its potential role as an alternative to PMGT5811 in the first-semester sequence (see Prerequisite Units — Group A).

    a Proposed unit merge
    🗣 Workshop discussion — proposed merge

    The group identified a potential merge involving PMGT5858, PMGT5820 and PMGT5871. The proposed merged unit name is still to be confirmed.

    The rationale for the merge is to check what is covered across these units and remove overlap. If progressed, the catalogue size would reduce from 22 units to 20 units.

    b Unit naming & content changes
    🗣 Workshop discussion — unit naming & content

    PMGT5205 — does not currently cover Professional Project Practice in the way the title may suggest. A name change should be explored: possible names discussed were Introduction to Professional Project Practice or Introduction to Project Management. The group also noted several professional practice concepts — including dilemmas and ethics — do not currently receive enough attention.

    PMGT5811 — identified as a difficult unit for first-semester students, particularly students from ESL backgrounds. There was discussion about whether it should move from core to elective status (see Core vs elective split), and whether the replacement core unit would still cover the necessary program and unit learning outcomes.

    PMGT5886 — discussed changing the name from System Dynamics Modelling for PM to Systems Thinking in Projects, and moving it from elective to core status (see Core vs elective split).

    PMGT5887 — discussed revamping the unit to incorporate AI and project technologies, alongside a possible name change.

    PMGT5895 — discussed whether Contracts Management should be renamed to incorporate Project Procurement.

    07 Take it away

    Summary & key risks

    A concise summary of the key decisions reached at the workshop, area by area, plus the key risks and considerations flagged for follow-up.

    ⚑ Key risks and considerations
    • Reduced unit availability may create larger classes.
    • Larger classes may create venue and timetabling constraints.
    • Any structural change must still support Graduate Certificate and Graduate Diploma pathways.
    • Curriculum sequencing needs further review to determine whether content should be delivered in a more scaffolded order.
    • Continuing academic ownership of units is important for curriculum quality and consistency.
    • Units taught without continuing academic ownership may be harder to manage.
    • Changes to PMGT5811 must preserve essential program learning outcomes and unit learning outcomes.
    • Changes to prerequisites must support student progression without allowing students to enrol in advanced units too early.
